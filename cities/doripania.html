17,000 - Small City

  • 75% Human
  • 15% Dwarven
  • 5% Halfling
  • 3% Elven
  • 2% Other

City focuses mostly on trade in and out of Maldantis, particularly with Lorn to the north and Kirhall to the west.

They are a significant merchant town and most of their income comes from trade and income tax from local and foreign merchants.

Economic Breakdown
  • Foreign Trade
  • River Work
  • Lumber
  • Agriculture
  • Artesian Goods

Almost all inhabitants speak common as a primary language with a distinct rural Maldant accent and dialect. Small Dwarven cloisters primarily speak Dwarvish.

The national religion of Maldantis is the Church of Taro. Other religions are permitted but ostracized and unrecognized.

Doripania falls under the rule of Folly and to a greater extent Maldantis and as such they are ruled by the Arch Pontiff Aralius. The government of Maldantis is a Theocracy.

  • Capinia: Hamlet located south of the city
  • Moesia Rivera: Fishing village and trade stop north along the Great Taran River.

The region relies heavily on the cereals of the mainland though they take heavily from the heavy meaty dishes of Kirhall and the roots and greens of the north. Doripania is known for their very well-rounded dishes and lavish culinary culture found in the upper-class of the city.

Biome: Temperate Steppe

The city finds itself in a small valley nestled between the hills to the north and the hills which cup the southern regions of the city, forming a wall between it and the southern forest.

Moderate tin and coal deposits in the northern hills.

The city is placed along the Great Taran River at a substantial crossing.

The region is temperate with colder winters due to its proximity to the river.

Forests consist mostly of various oaks and pines. Common crops are wheat, barley, and corn.

Local Plants
  • Bent Thorn: Small thistle type plant, very poisonous, often used in pest control poisons.

Most large magical beasts have been hunted to near extinction, what is left is common plains and woodland creatures, most notably boar and deer.

Local Animals
  • North Chester Root Hog: A wild boar known for its ability to find and dig roots.

Silty Clay Loam.
